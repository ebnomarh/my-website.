A visual history of the Kings of Saudi Arabia

Kings of Saudi Arabia

King Abdulaziz (Ibn Saud)

Reigned: 1932 – 1953

Founder of modern Saudi Arabia. United the regions into one kingdom in 1932.

King Saud

Reigned: 1953 – 1964

Led infrastructure development and expansion of education.

King Faisal

Reigned: 1964 – 1975

Modernized Saudi Arabia and was influential in the 1973 oil embargo.

King Khalid

Reigned: 1975 – 1982

Presided over rapid economic development and continued modernization.

King Fahd

Reigned: 1982 – 2005

Introduced the title "Custodian of the Two Holy Mosques" and oversaw the Gulf War response.

King Abdullah

Reigned: 2005 – 2015

Promoted educational reform, social modernization, and international diplomacy.

Future Leadership

King Salman

Reigned: 2015 – Present

Launched Vision 2030, transforming the Saudi economy and governance structure.

Crown Prince Mohammed bin Salman

Born: 1985

Architect of Vision 2030, focusing on economic diversification, tourism, and youth empowerment.
